# digitalcard — impeccable project context

## Design Context

### Users
Two audiences, one product (open-core):
- **Card creators** — professionals, freelancers, small businesses, and creators who want a
  shareable digital business card fast. They build mostly on **desktop**; their card is consumed by
  others on **mobile** (opened from a QR or a shared link). Jobs: assemble a card from blocks,
  pick/customize a theme, export a QR, copy a link, save contact (vCard).
- **Operators** — self-hosters deploying the OSS build, and hosted-plan users who get a larger
  theme catalog. Same engine; the catalog is the only difference.

### Brand Personality
Precise · quiet · crafted. The builder is a calm, confident **tool that gets out of the way** so the
user's card is the star. Professional, not playful. Refined, not flashy. It should feel like a
well-made instrument — every control deliberate, nothing shouting.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Minimal Swiss / Linear–Vercel.** Neutral zinc chrome, a single **indigo** accent
(`#5B5BD6` ≈ `oklch(0.53 0.19 279)`), strict 8px grid, generous whitespace, **borders over
shadows**, restrained motion. **Light mode default** (daytime desktop tool); full dark mode.
- Fonts: chrome = **Geist + Geist Mono** (NOT Inter — avoid monoculture; Geist is the truer Vercel
  match). Card fonts vary per theme — that is the expressive layer.
- Anti-references: gradient text, glassmorphism-everywhere, neon-on-dark "AI" look, cyan/purple
  hero gradients, busy dashboards, card-in-card nesting, colored side-stripe borders.

### Design Principles
1. **Two-layer separation.** Quiet, recessive chrome vs. expressive, themeable card. The chrome is
   a gallery wall; the card is the painting. Chrome never competes for attention.
2. **Theme is data.** One renderer; themes are objects. OSS and hosted differ only by catalog
   (`tier: 'oss' | 'hosted'`).
3. **Block-based composition.** One card model (ordered blocks) covers personal / business /
   link-in-bio / vCard. No separate templates.
4. **Borders & whitespace over decoration.** Hierarchy comes from weight, size, and space — not
   from color or shadow. Accent is rare (60-30-10).
5. **Accessible by default.** Chrome targets WCAG AA (AAA where cheap). Card colors are user-driven,
   so ship a **live contrast checker** rather than blocking customization.
